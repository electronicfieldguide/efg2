    This website was originally developed by Computer Science students at University Of Massachusetts Boston, as their final project for a Software Engineering course. Dr. Robert Morris was the project advisor and guide. Their client for this project was Dr. Robert Stevenson from the Biology department, also at UMass Boston.

    The students involved in the project were Daniel Keefe, Margaret Metcalf, Monisha Prakash, Dai Wu and Fang Yu.

    Dr. Morris and Dr. Stevenson followed up on the project and obtained an NSF grant to further the development of the Electronic Field Guide. Matthew Passell, a graduate student in Computer Science at UMass Boston has completely overhauled the system, which is primarily written in Java. So far, William Haber, a researcher, has provided us with valuable text data and images of butterflies and plants of Costa Rica.
